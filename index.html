About Me

I am a physics educator and STEM education specialist with experience designing learning environments across higher education and K–12 contexts. My work focuses on creating meaningful, technology-enhanced learning experiences that support deep conceptual understanding and student engagement.

With over a decade of experience teaching physics and designing STEM learning experiences, I have worked in university, secondary, and community-based educational settings in Chile and the United States. I specialize in translating complex scientific concepts into accessible, learner-centered experiences informed by educational research and learning data.

I approach teaching and instructional design as both a pedagogical and analytical practice, integrating evidence-informed strategies, digital tools, and reflective practice to support diverse learners. I am particularly interested in how data, technology, and culturally responsive approaches can expand access to high-quality STEM education.
